Project 7 - Book Cover

10 October, 2017

 



Watership Down is a classic novel by Richard Adams. Both the novel and the 1978 film adaptation are worth a look.

The main font is called American Captain, which I previously used on my postcard. It happened to fit quite well in this project. The back font is Futura Condensed, which has an early twentieth century vibe. The rabbits visit different societies, including one that is based on brutal fascism, which seems like a good match for Futura.

The biggest challenge for this project was finding suitable trees. I spent a couple of hours googling until I found trees with the perfect balance of eerie, but not too "Halloweeen." The trees are threatening as survival is a key theme in this novel. The colors are designed to complement each other, but also to create a sense of tension and danger. The red moon (or sun) is just a circle with a red fill. In the novel, the rabbits are traveling to a prophesied "promised land," and they travel by both day and night. The rabbit clipping mask has a picture of the canopy of some tree tops, as if you are looking up from inside a forest. The picture was inverted, so the tree branches are white, almost like bones.

The cover was inspired by the 1978 film adaptation, specifically the scene where the rabbits' warren is destroyed by machinery.
