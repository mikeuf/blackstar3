Final Project - IMC Campaign

5 December, 2017

 

This is a proposed IMC campaign for a company called "Roovillage."

Company Analysis

History
Roovillage (ROOVILLAGE, INC.) is a relatively new business, having been founded in June of 2016. They specialize in “drop-in” child care where they provide a font-end to an aggregation of multiple child care providers, allowing customers to find “affordable, licensed child care at a moment’s notice.” They are incorporated in the state of Delaware, but appear to be operating in California.

Target audience

  • The target audience is young professionals, between the ages of 21 and 50.
  • While it can be expected that women will comprise a majority of the customers, the campaign should avoid expressing this assumption to avoid causing offense.

Communication strategy
Key values:
  • Convenience
  • Accessibility
  • Trustworthiness

Using Roovillage to find an available child care opening is an additional cost to parents. Because of this, Roovillage must demonstrate that their service adds enough value to justify their take. They must clarify exactly what their added value is, from the perspective of a customer.

It appears that the key value Roovillage adds is convenience. Their service helps busy parents save time, and reduce stress, by quickly locating a child care opening. A related concept is accessibility. Roovillage must help parents save time, and also make it easy to use their scheduling tools. Additionally, they must be easy-to-reach for customer service queries and be responsive. This can be facilitated with a robust social media presence. At a minimum, representatives of Roovillage should be available to quickly answer questions, by phone, email, chat, direct tweets or Facebook queries.

Since Roovillage proposes to have parents allow them to decide where they should entrust their children, they must project an image of trustworthiness. Establishing trust can be done by creating website that is clean, attractive and professional. This image can be reinforced by advertising on child care related websites and forums.

Logo design
The orange and yellow convey an upbeat personality. The font is Omnes bold, from Adobe Typekit. It was chosen due to the rounded letters which convey a friendly and accessible image. A space has been inserted between the “Roo” and the “Village” to make it a bit easier to read and understand. Instead of drawing a realistic image of a kangaroo. The logo incorporates an implied kangaroo head that has a childlike and playful feel.

Note: The logo has been updated since the Project 9 submission to make the ears more closely resemble a kangaroo, instead of a rabbit. A new tagline ("Drop-in child care made easy") has also been added.


Print Promotions
This promotional flyer can be printed and posted or distributed in places where potential clients are likely to see them.

Suggested places include:
  • Office break rooms
  • Bulletin boards of other business in the area (with their permission)
  • Mailed or placed into the mailboxes of local residents

Social Media
Since Roovillage does not seem to currently have a “brick and mortar” location, maintaining an accessible presence on social media is critical. Being reachable for questions will help customers trust Roovillage and feel more comfortable recommending them to other parents.

As discussed in the company analysis, the target audience is mostly young women between the ages of 21 and 50. The best time to post content is during the early afternoon, during weekdays. This is a time when clickthroughs can be expected to be higher. Since the message is consistent, it should not be necessary to post new images more than once a week. Responding to queries from customers should be taking place continuously, however.

In addition to Facebook, Roovillage should maintain a Twitter account and be prepared to engage with customers. Pinterest is known to be a popular site for young women, so Roovillage should consider maintaining a presence there.
